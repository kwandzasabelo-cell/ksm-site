The Problem

The Industry Is Running on Guesswork and Losing Millions Because of It

Across Eswatini and the broader SADC freight market, operators are managing multi-million Lilangeni fleets with tools that were never designed for the job. WhatsApp threads, paper job cards, and spreadsheets that trail reality by weeks. The cost of this gap is not theoretical. It shows up in every fuel bill, every missed service, every job priced below cost.

// 01
Pricing Without Data
Jobs are quoted on instinct, not on cost models. The margin loss is invisible until month-end. By then it has already compounded across dozens of trips.
// 02
Fuel With No Accountability
Without per-trip tracking, there is no way to distinguish legitimate consumption from loss. The gap between what was bought and what the engine burned stays open indefinitely.
// 03
Maintenance as an Afterthought
Service intervals are tracked in someone's head, if at all. The breakdown on the N4 is not bad luck. It is the predictable result of reactive maintenance culture.
// 04
Compliance Flying Blind
Roadworthy certificates, cross-border permits, PDPs. They expire quietly. The fine or the impound does not arrive with a warning.
// 05
Financials That Are Always Late
Month-end reporting consumes days of effort and still does not produce a clear picture. Profitability per truck, per route, per driver: unknown.
// 06
No Driver Visibility
What happened on the road stays on the road. Without structured logging, every trip is a black box and accountability is impossible to enforce.
The Platform

Engineered for Southern African Freight. Powered by AI.

KSM Smart Freight Solutions is a web-based fleet intelligence platform built ground-up for the realities of operating in Eswatini and across SADC corridors, local fuel pricing structures, fleet card integrations, cross-border compliance, and the routes that connect this region to its markets.

The platform integrates real-time market data, AI-powered decision support, and structured operational logging into a single interface accessible from any device, giving fleet managers and drivers exactly what they need, and nothing they do not.

It is not a generic fleet tracker adapted for Africa. It is built here, for here, with the corridors, the compliance frameworks, the fuel pricing, and the border crossings already embedded in the system.
